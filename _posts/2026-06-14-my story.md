Current Outbreak Status

A new outbreak of Ebola disease caused by the Bundibugyo virus was confirmed in the DRC in May 2026. On May 17, 2026, the World Health Organization (WHO) declared the outbreak a Public Health Emergency of International Concern.

As of June 12, 2026, the DRC Ministry of Health and WHO have reported:

676 confirmed cases

136 confirmed deaths

The outbreak is primarily affecting the eastern provinces of Ituri, North Kivu, and South Kivu, spanning 34 health zones.

Cross-Border Transmission
The outbreak has also spread to neighboring Uganda, which has reported 19 confirmed cases and 2 deaths, primarily linked to travel from the DRC and subsequent secondary transmission among contacts and healthcare workers.

Challenges in the Response

Lack of Targeted Countermeasures: Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, the Bundibugyo strain currently has no licensed vaccines or specific FDA-approved therapeutics.

Humanitarian Crisis: The outbreak is occurring in a region heavily impacted by ongoing armed conflict, population displacement, and dense refugee camps. Collapsing health services and insecurity are severely hindering surveillance, contact tracing, and the delivery of medical supplies.

Healthcare Worker Impact: The virus initially clustered among healthcare workers, and several have died during this outbreak. The WHO and Africa CDC have launched a joint continental response plan to scale up clinical preparedness, cross-border screening, and community engagement.
